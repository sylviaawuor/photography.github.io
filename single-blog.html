SPLISH SPLASH IN THE POOL

DECEMBER 11th 2017

Let's Talk

The first 12 months of your baby's life flashes in a whizz and is fully loaded with milestones, loads of memories and many firsts. I'm sure the evidences are still in your phone's photo gallery. The calendar weeks fly by so fast that even the best laid plans like doing a professional photo shoot never see the light of day. However, there are so many firsts you can do with your baby before his/her first birthday.

Babies get amazed by the tiniest of things, from discovering their tiny sweet chubby fingers, to the tiniest speck of food that found itself on their finger. Simple things like these could possibly keep them busy for minutes. It's the tiniest little things that literally blow their little minds off. This is the best time to take advantage of their quick to learn nature and teach them basic safe water skills.

I used to think that babies are born with the ability to swim, but later, found out that that's not true. The only thing they have are reflexes that make it look like they are. Babies aren't old enough to hold their breath intentionally or strong enough to keep their head above water. Although this may be taught to reduce their risk of drowning especially if you have a pool around. It is extremely important to note that while babies can imitate swimming motions and reflexes, they are not yet physically capable of swimming.

Most babies love to spend time in water, hence, they would definitely enjoy swimming. Apparently, swimming is also recommended for their physical and moto-sensoric development, social skills and bonding. In-Case you are looking for activities to enrol your baby, I would recommend you register your baby for a 6 weeks course at the Nairobi Water Babies to experience professional, child-centered baby swimming classes by an amazing instructor called Lisa. They take babies from 4 Months to 3 years. During the classes, you get to sing, play and do all sorts of physical exercises of balance and safety.

The end result, is to teach babies to be confident in water and learn how to pull themselves to safety in case of an accident. The pool is well heated to 35 Degrees, so you don't need to worry about your baby catching a cold.
