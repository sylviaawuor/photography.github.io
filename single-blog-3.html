Nanny from hell

DECEMBER 11th 2017

Let's Talk

9 months later after I found out I was pregnant, the cold rainy morning of 19.02.2017, that was the first time I had my baby's cry. I remember crying tears of joy uncontrollably and thanking God over and over again for the beautiful gift he had bestowed on me.

As I stared to the hospital ceiling, I was extremely happy and completely clueless of what awaited me at the end of this journey. I had read so much, but what I experienced after birth was nowhere in the paragraphs I read.

Day by day, I learned, I faulted, I laughed, I smiled, I cried over and over again. All these small things wrapped up to this immense love that I have for this little angel. Anyone who is a mother would know what I'm talking about.

Before delivery date, my husband and I had spent hours on end looking for a perfect nanny for our baby. We had a list of what we figured would work well for us. We were even willing to spend a little more just to get the best there was. We conducted several interviews until we spotted one, who we felt was the best fit.

Days turned into weeks, then to months, but having to say goodbye to my little one every morning was always a heartbreaker. The most heartbreaking moment was when I came home in the evening and as I played with her and the nanny was leaving, she cried for her. Any mother out there, would've gotten emotional about this. Anyone who knows me well, would tell you Shama can literally cry for the world!

I had hardly gotten over that, than what I'm about to share happened. This is the most tormenting feeling a mother can ever have. Subscribe, to find out my biggest nightmare and what pushed me to be where I am.
